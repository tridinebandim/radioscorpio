            [info] => Maria McKee (born Maria Louise McKee, on August 17, 1964 in Los Angeles, California) is an American singer.
 
 A solo artist, McKee was a founding member of the cowpunk/country rock band <a href="http://www.last.fm/music/Lone+Justice" class="bbcode_artist">Lone Justice</a>, in 1982, with whom she released two albums. Her band opened for such acts as U2.
 
 In 1986, she wrote <a href="http://www.last.fm/music/Feargal+Sharkey" class="bbcode_artist">Feargal Sharkey</a>'s UK number one hit 'A Good Heart'.
 
 She released her first solo, self-titled album in 1989. Her song &quot;Show Me Heaven&quot;, which appeared on the soundtrack to the film Days of Thunder, was a number one single in the United Kingdom for four weeks in 1990. Later her eerie, country-tinged single, &quot;If Love Is a Red Dress (Hang Me in Rags)&quot; was released on the soundtrack of Pulp Fiction. (1994).
 
 Her recorded output has varied between the rootsy country-folk of You Gotta Sin To Get Saved (1993) and Peddlin' Dreams  (2005), and experimental rock on Life Is Sweet (1996) and High Dive (2003).  She  even made a foray into early-90s dance music with her &quot;Sweetest Child&quot; single.
 
 Life Is Sweet was seen at the time as an artistic about-face from her country-rock sound, full of guitar feedback and complex, baroque songwriting. Confounding both fans and critics, it was her last release for seven years and her last for a major label.
 
 In 2007 she released Late December, an album rooted in classic 60s pop songwriting.
 
 She is the half-sister of <a href="http://www.last.fm/music/Love" class="bbcode_artist">Love</a> guitarist/singer/songwriter <a href="http://www.last.fm/music/Bryan+Maclean" class="bbcode_artist">Bryan Maclean</a>, with whom she played in a duo as a teenager. In the nineties, she spent time living in Dublin and the East Village.
